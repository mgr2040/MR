Bio

I am interested in understanding rock and fracture mecahnics, including mechanical and transport phenomena of fluid and heat in areas of geothermal energy, deep geological sequestration of CO2, and unconventional hydrocarbons. My research focuses on the use of experiemental methods and machine learning to bring light to the many problems we face in harvesting Earth's energy and reducing environmental risk.

Fluid-Induced Seismicity

Fluid-induced shear reactivation of pre-stressed faults poses a project terminating risk during reservoir stimulation in geothermal, CO2 sequestration, wastewater disposal injection and hydraulic fracturing operations. Micro-earthquakes (MEQs) can be tolerated but fluid injection is capable of triggering large earthquakes that can cause major damage to surface infrastructure. Understanding the linkage between fluid-injection volume or injection rate and seismic moment is essential in safeguarding such infrastructure.

Aquifer Thermal Energy Storage Systems

Utilizing aquifer thermal energy storage systems (ATES) for seasonal space heating and cooling in public and commercial spaces is one solution to reduce fossil fuel consumption for energy and heat production. This study investigates appropriate numerical methods that aim to reduce residual heat related to low-temperature thermal energy storage systems deployed in groundwater aquifers. Limited studies exist that assess methods for reducing thermal energy stored in groundwater aquifers to achieve pre-ATES deployment conditions. So, the goal is to understand thermal dispersion in the aquifer and heat loss to the confining layers for up to 30 years post operation. My question: How effective is cold-water injection during push-pull testing in reducing residual heat in an aquifer?
